This is a story that started three years ago. I participated in a workshop at my local UNI about AI safety and was confronted for the first time with a lot of interesting topics such as alignment and AI reward hacking as well as other technical aspects. But with it came also some deeper discussions and some at that time seemingly unbelievable conversations about how that would actually play out with large language models. GPT-3.5-turbo, commonly known as the original ChatGPT, had been released a year before that.

There are a lot of discussions to be had around "AI"; they could be about economical speculation risks, impact on job markets, and environmental and ethical concerns. But all of this is completely irrelevant and does not matter whatsoever if we don't focus on "AI" safety first. THIS is what should matter now. The systems that are being built by tech corporations (OpenAI, Anthropic, Alibaba, Microsoft, Google, SpaceX, Palantir, Microsoft, and Meta, among others, with these being the most prominent), sort of Frankenstein machines with capabilities being attached to them on all fronts, are all converging to become a threat to humanity as a whole. There is no other way to frame this, and I'm not wanting to lighten it either.

This might come off as exaggerated, or I might lack, in your eyes, the required expertise on the subject to legitimize such a claim. I am not asking you to accept it as is either. The goal of this post is not to give you sources on every sentence or teach you the subject, but I will do my best to leave you a collection of resources below to learn more by yourself.

It is OUR *collective* responsibility to go forth and protect humanity. And to be clear, the issue here is not necessarily the "AI takeover" scenario we were told about by books and movies. It might as well come from power getting into the wrong hands: mass spreading of manipulative information and development of deadly weapons, as well as plain and simple accidents, just to name a few. The point being that the issue is not necessarily some "AI being mean," and so it would not be "AI thinking humanity is not worth existing on earth." This can also lead to interesting discussions about what specifically makes humanity so rich and (optionally) worth existing. It is a discussion I am keen to have, but not here and now.

YOUR own role is now to act on this. This is information that cannot be ignored, no matter what you think. For you personally, this might mean doing many things, but the most impactful today are sharing this information with as many people as you can and acting on this on the political level, to the extent of your abilities in that matter, of course. I genuinely believe that a good ending can be achieved, but it will and does require real people to act in the real world.
